# Comprehensive System Development Intake Specification

This defines the complete intake needed to develop any system (partnership, product, market entry, etc.)

## 1. IDENTITY & FOUNDATION (Intake Step 1)
**What**: Define WHO you are and what you're bringing to the table

### 1.1 Organization Core
- [ ] Legal Entity Name & Registration
- [ ] Entity Type (Public/Private/Startup/NGO/Academic)
- [ ] Industry Classification (NAICS/SIC codes)
- [ ] Years in Operation
- [ ] Headquarters Location (Country, State/Province, City)
- [ ] Operating Regions (where currently active)

### 1.2 Organizational Capacity
- [ ] Total Employees (Headcount Band + Exact Number)
- [ ] Annual Revenue (Band + Exact or Estimated)
- [ ] EBITDA / Net Income
- [ ] Key Business Segments (% breakdown)
- [ ] Market Share in Primary Segment
- [ ] Profitability Trend (growing/stable/declining)

### 1.3 Organizational Structure
- [ ] Decision-Making Authority (CEO/Board/Committee)
- [ ] Key Decision Makers (Names, Titles, Contact)
- [ ] Organizational Chart (simplified)
- [ ] Board Composition (if applicable)
- [ ] Key Advisors / Strategic Partners

### 1.4 Competitive Position
- [ ] Primary Competitors (3-5)
- [ ] Competitive Advantages (Core)
- [ ] Unique IP / Technology / Moat
- [ ] Brand Recognition (Regional/National/Global)
- [ ] Customer Concentration Risk

### 1.5 Financial Stability
- [ ] Credit Rating / Payment History
- [ ] Bank References
- [ ] Audit Status (audited/reviewed/unaudited)
- [ ] Debt Levels (Leverage Ratio)
- [ ] Cash Position / Liquidity

## 2. MANDATE & STRATEGY (Intake Step 2)
**What**: Why you're here, what you want to achieve, and how you'll measure it

### 2.1 Strategic Vision
- [ ] Long-term Vision (3-5 year outlook)
- [ ] Primary Strategic Intent(s) (select all applicable)
  - [ ] Market Expansion (new geographies)
  - [ ] Product/Service Innovation
  - [ ] Capability Acquisition (technology, talent, IP)
  - [ ] Revenue Growth (organic vs. inorganic)
  - [ ] Operational Efficiency
  - [ ] Risk Mitigation
  - [ ] ESG/Sustainability Goals

### 2.2 Core Problem Statement
- [ ] Problem We're Solving (specific, measurable)
- [ ] Current State (what exists today)
- [ ] Desired Future State (what success looks like)
- [ ] Why Now (urgency, market conditions)
- [ ] Cost of Inaction

### 2.3 Objectives & KPIs
- [ ] Top 3 Strategic Objectives
- [ ] Primary KPI (the one number that matters most)
- [ ] Secondary KPIs (3-5)
- [ ] Success Timeline (by when)
- [ ] Measurement Methodology

### 2.4 Target Partner Profile
- [ ] Ideal Partner Size (revenue range, headcount)
- [ ] Partner Industry/Segment
- [ ] Partner Geography
- [ ] Partner Capabilities Needed
- [ ] Partner Culture/Values Fit
- [ ] Decision-Maker Profile

### 2.5 Value Proposition
- [ ] What We Bring (quantified)
- [ ] What We Expect (quantified)
- [ ] Win-Win Framework (specific to each party)
- [ ] Risk Share (who bears what)
- [ ] Upside Share (profit split, equity stake)

### 2.6 Governance & Integration
- [ ] Preferred Structure (JV / Alliance / Equity / License / Distribution)
- [ ] Governance Model (voting, veto rights, board seats)
- [ ] Decision Process (monthly/quarterly meetings)
- [ ] Escalation Path (for disputes)
- [ ] Integration Timeline
- [ ] Integration Lead (who owns this)

## 3. MARKET & CONTEXT (Intake Step 3)
**What**: WHERE you're operating and what forces are at play

### 3.1 Market Definition
- [ ] TAM (Total Addressable Market) in $ and units
- [ ] SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market) 
- [ ] SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)
- [ ] Market Growth Rate (CAGR %)
- [ ] Market Maturity (emerging/growth/mature/decline)
- [ ] Key Market Segments & Mix

### 3.2 Market Dynamics
- [ ] Top 5 Market Trends
- [ ] Technology Disruption Threats
- [ ] Regulatory Headwinds/Tailwinds
- [ ] Price Elasticity of Demand
- [ ] Customer Concentration (top customer % of revenue)
- [ ] Buyer Decision Cycle (days)

### 3.3 Geographic Context
- [ ] Target Country (primary)
- [ ] Target Region/State
- [ ] Target City(ies)
- [ ] Local Market Size ($M)
- [ ] Competition in Local Market (# competitors)
- [ ] Import/Export Regulations
- [ ] Tax Treaty Status
- [ ] IP Protection Quality
- [ ] Geopolitical Risk Score

### 3.4 Macroeconomic Factors
- [ ] GDP Growth (target country, 3-year forecast)
- [ ] Inflation Rate
- [ ] Currency Volatility Risk
- [ ] Interest Rate Environment
- [ ] Trade Policy (tariffs, sanctions)
- [ ] Labor Cost Trends

### 3.5 Industry-Specific Dynamics
- [ ] Consolidation Trend (is industry consolidating?)
- [ ] Pricing Power (pricing pressure or margin expansion)
- [ ] Supplier Power (concentrated or fragmented)
- [ ] Customer Power (concentrated or fragmented)
- [ ] Switching Costs (low/medium/high)
- [ ] Barrier to Entry (low/medium/high)

## 4. PARTNERS & ECOSYSTEM (Intake Step 4)
**What**: WHO else will be involved and how do relationships work

### 4.1 Target Counterparties
- [ ] Partner Name(s) (primary, secondary)
- [ ] Partner Organization Size
- [ ] Partner Revenue
- [ ] Partner Core Capabilities
- [ ] Partner Geographic Footprint
- [ ] Partner Strategic Direction
- [ ] Contact at Partner (CEO/CFO/COO level)

### 4.2 Stakeholder Landscape
- [ ] Executive Stakeholders (who needs to approve)
- [ ] Operational Stakeholders (who implements)
- [ ] Financial Stakeholders (CFO, Controller)
- [ ] Legal/Compliance Stakeholders
- [ ] Board/Investor Interests
- [ ] Customer Impact (will this affect relationships)
- [ ] Employee Impact (retention risk)

### 4.3 Alignment Assessment
- [ ] Strategic Alignment Score (1-10)
  - Our goals vs. Partner goals
  - Our timeline vs. Partner timeline
  - Our risk tolerance vs. Partner risk tolerance
- [ ] Cultural Fit Assessment
  - Decision-making speed
  - Hierarchy (flat vs. hierarchical)
  - Innovation appetite
  - Risk aversion profile
  - Communication style

### 4.4 Competitive Conflicts
- [ ] Do we compete with partner? (Yes/No)
- [ ] Do we compete with partner's customers? (Yes/No)
- [ ] Do we compete with partner's suppliers? (Yes/No)
- [ ] Non-compete Implications
- [ ] Channel Conflict Risk

### 4.5 Relationship Dynamics
- [ ] Relationship History (new/existing/previous)
- [ ] Trust Level (1-10)
- [ ] Prior Dealings (successful/mixed/failed)
- [ ] Relationship Depth (1 contact / multiple / deep)
- [ ] Communication Cadence (expected)
- [ ] Deal Experience (have they done deals like this)

## 5. FINANCIAL MODEL (Intake Step 5)
**What**: The money - investment, returns, timing, and sensitivity

### 5.1 Investment Requirements
- [ ] Capital Investment Needed ($)
- [ ] Investment Type (equity/debt/grant/sweat)
- [ ] Investment Timing (upfront / phased)
- [ ] Working Capital Needed ($)
- [ ] Contingency Buffer (% of budget)
- [ ] Funding Sources (where will $ come from)
- [ ] Funding Secured ($) vs. Planned ($)

### 5.2 Revenue Model
- [ ] Revenue Stream 1: [Name] - $ per [unit], [# units/year] = $X
- [ ] Revenue Stream 2: [Name] - $ per [unit], [# units/year] = $X
- [ ] Revenue Stream 3: [Name] - $ per [unit], [# units/year] = $X
- [ ] Year 1 Revenue (Total)
- [ ] Year 3 Revenue (Total)
- [ ] Year 5 Revenue (Total)
- [ ] Revenue Growth Rate (%, Year 1-3)
- [ ] Recurring vs. One-time (%)

### 5.3 Cost Structure
- [ ] COGS as % of Revenue
- [ ] Gross Margin Target
- [ ] Operating Expense Budget (fixed + variable)
- [ ] Head Count Plan (Year 1, 3, 5)
- [ ] Key Cost Drivers
- [ ] Cost Reduction Opportunities

### 5.4 Return Analysis
- [ ] Break-even Timeline (months to positive cash flow)
- [ ] Year 5 EBITDA Margin (%)
- [ ] Net Present Value (NPV @ discount rate)
- [ ] Internal Rate of Return (IRR)
- [ ] Return Multiple (total cash returned / invested)
- [ ] Payback Period (months)
- [ ] ROIC (Return on Invested Capital) %)

### 5.5 Sensitivity & Scenarios
- [ ] Base Case Assumption (what has to be true)
- [ ] Downside Case (25% revenue miss)
- [ ] Upside Case (25% revenue beat)
- [ ] Key Sensitivity Drivers (top 3 risks to returns)
